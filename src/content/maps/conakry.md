## The Geography

Conakry focuses on the coast of Guinea and its surrounding West African approaches. The map is wide at 2456x1000, with 45% land and 55% water. That balance gives the battlefield a coastal rhythm: land expansion is real, but the ocean is close enough to interrupt almost every plan.

The real city of Conakry sits on the Kaloum Peninsula and nearby islands. On the map, that geography becomes a lesson in peninsulas, river mouths, and coastal reach. Players who control the shoreline can threaten inland rivals from unexpected angles.

## The History

### Precolonial West African Networks
The region around modern Guinea was connected to Manding, Fula, and broader West African trade and political systems long before European colonial rule.

### 1880s - French Colonial Expansion
France developed Conakry as a colonial port. Its location made it a gateway for administration, exports, and Atlantic shipping.

### 1958 - Guinean Independence
Guinea voted for immediate independence from France in 1958, becoming one of the first French colonies in sub-Saharan Africa to break away.

## The Battlefield

### Terrain Overview
Conakry has 20 nations and a near-even split between land and water. The map is not purely naval, but a player without coastal awareness will be punished.

### Best Spawns
- **Peninsula-adjacent starts** - strong access to both sea lanes and inland routes.
- **Broad inland pockets** - safer economic growth before contesting the coast.
- **River-mouth positions** - useful for controlling movement between land and water.

### Avoid
- **Thin coastal strips** - easy to raid and hard to reinforce.
- **Deep inland tunnel vision** - the coast can become an invasion highway if left alone.

### Strategic Insights
Conakry rewards players who synchronize land pushes with sea denial. A secure port can multiply your reach, but a port without an inland base is just a target. Build both.

## Fun Facts
- Conakry is Guinea's capital and largest city.
- The city's port is central to Guinea's economy.
- The map's shape makes it one of the more horizontal regional battlefields in the roster.
